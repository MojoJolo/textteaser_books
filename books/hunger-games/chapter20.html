Chapter 20

  • I left the scene of his last hiding place on the bank of the stream untouched how could I conceal it?
  • I'm just going to have to trust that whatever instinct sent me to find him was a good one.
  • When the sky turns rosy, I notice the sheen of sweat on Peetas lip and discover the fever has broken.
  • I reach out to touch his cheek and he catches my hand and presses it against his lips.
  • I remember my father doing this very thing to my mother and I wonder where Peeta picked it up.
  • We get him propped up against the wall and he obediently swallows the spoonfuls of the berry mush I feed him.
  • And probably better to do it now when he seems relatively alert and we have daylight on our side.
  • Peeta sits beside me, leaning against the wall, his bad leg stretched out before him, his eyes trained on the world outside.
  • I give him more fever pills and stand over him while he drinks first one, then a second quart of water.
  • I swear the Gamemakers are progressively ratcheting up the temperature in the daytime and sending it plummeting at night.
