The Hunger Games

Chapter 2

  • Someone is gripping my arm, a boy from the Seam, and I think maybe I started to fall and he caught me.
  • In some districts, in which winning the reaping is such a great honor, people are eager to risk their lives, the volunteering is complicated.
  • But in District 12, where the word tribute is pretty much synonymous with the word corpse, volunteers are all but extinct.
  • Prim, let go, I say harshly, because this is upsetting me and I dont want to cry.
  • When they televise the replay of the reapings tonight, everyone will make note of my tears, and I'll be marked as an easy target.
  • At least, I dont expect it because I don't think of District 12 as a place that cares about me.
  • At school, I passed the boy in the hall, his cheek had swelled up and his eye had blackened.
  • But as I collected Prim and started for home that afternoon, I found him staring at me from across the schoolyard.
  • I thought of the hours spent in the woods with my father and I knew how we were going to survive.
  • Peeta looks me right in the eye and gives my hand what I think is meant to be a reassuring squeeze.
